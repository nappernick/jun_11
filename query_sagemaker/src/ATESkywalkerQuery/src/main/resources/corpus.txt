What Am I Allowed to Book?
Preferred suppliers are identified in the Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool. Book accommodations near your work destination through the Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool. Amazon reimburses the lowest refundable standard hotel room rate on a single-occupancy basis.

Start with the Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool—it's the quickest way to modify your reservation. Most hotel changes can be handled directly through self-service.
Before modifying, confirm your reservation meets these criteria:
•	You have not checked into the hotel
•	The request falls within the cancel policy guidelines
•	The hotel has not been paid for in advance 

For travelers based in most countries (including U.S.):
•	Log in to the Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool 
•	Go to Trips > Upcoming. Select your trip - Find your hotel reservation and click Modify (or Cancel)
•	Either change your dates by editing the check-in and check-out fields and clicking Check Availability, or select a different room type or rate
•	Review the changes and price differences on the Checkout page
•	Click Book Hotel to confirm your modifications
•	Watch this quick video: Modify Your Flight & Hotel 

For travelers based in specific regions (Bahrain, Brazil, China, Egypt, India, Israel, Japan, Jordan, Kenya, Kuwait, Mexico, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Spain, and United Arab Emirates):

There are two types of hotel changes: 
1.	Modify the dates of your current hotel reservation
2.	Book an entirely new hotel (requires cancellation of the original reservation)

In both cases, book the new hotel or new dates first, then cancel the old reservation. This method ensures you do not lose the current reservation if the hotel of choice is sold out. 

6.	Am I allowed to upgrade my fare class or air fare?

Yes, but Amazon will only reimburse the lowest logical fare in economy class. You'll need to pay the difference between the lowest logical policy-approved fare and the upgraded class out of pocket.

Here's how to arrange it:

For travelers based in most countries (including U.S.):
•	Search and select your preferred upgraded cabin class in the Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool (desktop only)—you'll notice the policy-approved amount and personal upgrade cost displayed in the fare indicator.
•	On the Out of Policy pop-up, select Skip suggestions and proceed to the checkout page, then click Next, On the Checkout page 
•	Review the breakdown of the two amounts
•	Confirm your personal card is defaulted as the form of payment
•	Acknowledge the booking contains a personal upgrade
•	Click Book flight to finalize your reservation

As a reminder, you'll pay the full amount upfront and submit the policy-approved portion for 		reimbursement. The invoice/itinerary you receive will include a Personal Upgrade Summary for your 	expense report. Need help? Check out this quick video. 

For travelers based in specific regions (Bahrain, Brazil, China, Egypt, India, Israel, Japan, Jordan, Kenya, Kuwait, Mexico, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Spain and United Arab Emirates):
•	Contact an Amazon Travel Counselor and let them know you'd like to add a personal upgrade to your trip.
•	You'll pay for the entire ticket cost upfront using your own payment method. Make sure to get documentation showing the price difference between lowest logical policy-approved class fare and the upgraded class—you'll need this when filing your expense report.
•	When you expense your trip, only claim reimbursement for the documented lowest logical fare noted by the travel services provider. Keep in mind that upgrade fees and premium seating fees are not reimbursable.
•	Refer to the Global Travel and Expense Policy for more information. 

Note: Your manager will receive an email notification about your booking, which will indicate you've added a personal upgrade.

28. What size rental car am I allowed to book? 
Travelers can book up to intermediate size vehicles, including mini, economy, and compact sizes, through the Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool or Designated Travel Counselors.

Where available, Amazon encourages the use of electric or hybrid rental vehicles. When the only electric or hybrid vehicles available are standard or full-size, these may be used instead of an intermediate size vehicle.

Exceptions for Larger Vehicles

•	Three (3) or more travelers traveling together and sharing the rental vehicle

•	Pre-approved medical reasons

Travelers should consider the risks associated with driving, especially outside their home country and after long flights.
•	Driver Requirements:
o	Hold a driving license suitable for driving the type of car rented and permitted by local law
o	Be familiar with local driving conditions before renting
•	Risk Management:
o	Car rental is not advised unless the driver is familiar with local conditions
o	Follow Travel Risk Management (TRM) ground transportation recommendations for elevated risk destinations in TRM advisories

29. Why is my booking out of policy?
The Out of Policy flag helps travelers identify the best options that comply with the Global Travel and Expense Policy, which considers:
•	Lowest logical economy fare with a seat
•	Lowest hotel rates
•	Preferred car rates for the approved car type

Out of Policy vs. Policy Violation
Out of Policy:
•	Does not prevent you from booking for business purposes
•	Requires you to provide a reason for your selection (for example, a hotel that may show out of policy but is closer to your business visits, or booking an intermediate SUV during inclement weather)
•	Serves as an audit tool to ensure flexibility while maintaining safety and frugality
Policy Violation:
•	The travel program has rules that prevent policy violations (for example, booking a cabin in first class)
•	These bookings cannot be completed

Signing authority: Only select individuals are authorized to sign on behalf of the company. 39. Can I get permission to upgrade for long-haul flights?

Amazon has a defined process for granting cabin upgrade exceptions for travelers who frequently fly long-haul international trips. Travelers who meet specific criteria may be approved to receive Premium Economy Accommodation (PEA), an exception to the Travel and Expense policy.

Eligibility Requirements
Once you qualify based on the criteria below, you will be eligible to self-book premium economy cabin for flights that are 14 hours or more, excluding layovers. PEA exceptions do not apply to all flights.

To qualify for the PEA list, you must:
•	Book all travel through the Amazon Travel Program using either the Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool or an Amazon travel counselor
•	Complete four (4) or more one-way trips exceeding 14 hours (excluding layover time) in the last 12 months

How the PEA List Works
No action required from you. Once you fulfill the minimum requirements, your name will be automatically added to the PEA list.

Employees who qualify for the Premium Economy List will have their Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool automatically updated to allow booking upgraded premium economy flights.

Status monitoring:
•	Qualifications are monitored daily
•	Once minimum criteria are fulfilled, travelers maintain status for one year from the qualifying date
•	No manual review or employee action required
•	Open a SIM-T request so that we can verify PEA status but cannot provide exceptions

41. Why Am I Receiving a New Hotel Confirmation? I Didn't Make Any Changes to My Hotel Booking
Amazon Travel & Events uses automated auditing technology that continuously monitors your hotel reservations after booking to find lower rates. When a better rate becomes available, the system automatically rebooks your hotel to capture those savings for you.

What stays the same:
•	Your hotel property
•	Your check-in and check-out dates
•	Your room type and preferences
•	All other reservation details

What changes:
•	Your confirmation number (you'll receive a new one)
•	Your rate (it will be lower!)

Why This Happens
Hotel rates fluctuate frequently due to demand, cancellations, and promotional pricing. Our automated system works behind the scenes to spot these price drops and secure the savings automatically—so you don't have to monitor rates yourself or manually rebook.

Important Notes
-	No action needed from you—the rebooking happens automatically
-	Some exemptions may apply based on specific booking circumstances
-	Always use your new confirmation number when checking in or contacting the hotel


42. Why can’t I see the Amazon form of payment when booking my flight?
If you cannot select the Amazon form of payment (Amazon Air central bill), your booking likely includes one or more of the following:
-	Personal upgrades (e.g., premium cabin, extra legroom)
-	Seat selection fees
-	Baggage fees
-	Other ancillary charges
-	Personal travel segments

Why this matters: The Amazon Self-Service Booking Tool does not support the Amazon form of payment for personal add-ons or ancillary services. These must be paid using an alternate payment method.

Additional consideration: Some airlines require multi-factor authentication for payment processing, which also requires an alternate form of payment.

Need help? If you believe you should have access to the Amazon form of payment and none of these conditions apply, contact Customer Support for assistance.
